What is Intel?

Founded in 1968, Intel Corporation is one of the world's largest and most influential semiconductor companies. Headquartered in Santa Clara, California, Intel designs and manufactures the microprocessors and chips that power billions of computers, servers, smartphones, and connected devices globally.

From the iconic Intel Core processors inside laptops to the Xeon chips running cloud data centers — Intel's silicon is the engine behind modern computing. They also develop AI accelerators, networking hardware, and cutting-edge fabrication technology at their own chip factories called "fabs."

Sustainability Timeline

2008

First Carbon-Neutral Goal Set

Intel sets its first target to reduce absolute greenhouse gas emissions across all global operations.

// First in semiconductor industry to formalize carbon targets before regulatory requirements — a voluntary commitment that reshaped internal engineering priorities.

#climate
2012

Water Restoration Programs Launch

Intel pledges to restore 100% of freshwater used globally — the first semiconductor company to do so.

// Partnership with local watershed conservation groups funded restoration projects exceeding Intel's annual water draw. Model later adopted by 3 competitor companies.

#water
2015

Zero Waste to Landfill Achieved

All major Intel manufacturing sites reach zero-waste-to-landfill status worldwide.

// Over 75% of manufacturing byproducts recycled or converted to energy. Chemical waste recovery programs diverted 3,000+ tons annually from landfills.

#waste

100% Renewable Electricity — U.S.

All U.S. Intel operations powered by 100% renewable electricity via direct purchases and RECs.

// Intel became one of the largest U.S. green power purchasers. Wind + solar sourcing equivalent to powering 200,000 homes — validated by EPA Green Power Partnership.

RISE Strategy Unveiled

Intel launches RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) with bold 2030 targets.

// RISE quantified every target into measurable KPIs auditable by third parties — a departure from vague "commitments" to engineering-grade accountability.

#strategy

Net-Zero GHG by 2040 Pledged

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its entire global value chain by 2040.
